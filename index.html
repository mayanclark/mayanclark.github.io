About me

Geographer, analyst,
and land advocate.

I'm Maya Clark — a geospatial analyst and environmental researcher based in West Virginia with a B.S. in Geography and Environmental Planning (GIS minor) from Towson University. My work lives at the intersection of satellite data, watershed science, and community land stewardship.

I've led remote sensing projects at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center, analyzed oil slick detections for SkyTruth, mapped fisheries data across 12 West African nations for CIEL, and monitored Chesapeake Bay ecosystems for the Chesapeake Bay Foundation. I'm also rooted in regenerative agriculture — helping grow Sacred Roots Herbal Sanctuary in Shepherdstown, WV.

Whether you need a watershed delineation, a grant proposal, or a map that makes your data sing — I can help.

NASA DEVELOP · Remote Sensing

Land & Water Resource Monitoring from Space

SkyTruth · Oil Pollution Detection

Cerulean API Oil Slick Detection Pipeline

Built and debugged an automated pipeline querying the Cerulean API for satellite-detected oil slicks by vessel MMSI. Resolved complex API challenges and delivered clean geospatial datasets for environmental accountability.

Python Cerulean API GeoPandas SAR Imagery

CIEL · Fisheries Advocacy

West Africa Fisheries Data Atlas

Compiled and visualized Sea Around Us catch data across 12 West African nations for 4 key species. Produced choropleth and bubble maps using Marine Regions EEZ polygons — supporting advocacy against offshore oil expansion ahead of a major Cabo Verde conference.

R ggplot2 Sea Around Us EEZ polygons

Chesapeake Bay Foundation · Watershed GIS

Bay Ecosystem & Watershed Mapping

Applied GIS analysis to support monitoring and advocacy for one of North America's most complex estuarine systems. Developed spatial datasets supporting CBF's conservation and policy work across the Chesapeake watershed.

ArcGIS Pro Watershed Analysis Environmental Monitoring

SkyTruth · Water Quality Research

Data Center Water Quality Impact Study

Leading a feasibility sprint assessing whether ambient water quality monitoring data can detect pollution signals near data centers. Includes DEM-based watershed delineation, upstream/downstream gage pair identification, and spatial analysis in ArcGIS Pro.
