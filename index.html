Independent Research

Canopy in Crisis
Baltimore's urban trees are disappearing — how can we respond?
$5,000 CLA Summer Research Grant · Towson University · 2025

Where Baltimore's tree canopy is disappearing — and why

Analyzed 11 years of Landsat and U.S. Forest Service canopy data, finding a 17% decline in Baltimore's tree cover. Population density and vacant housing emerged as the strongest predictors of loss — exposing its environmental justice dimensions. Awarded Best Paper at the AAG Mid-Atlantic Division Conference.

About me

Community advocate,
powered by data.

I'm Maya Clark — a community advocate and geospatial analyst with a B.S. in Geography and Environmental Planning (Geographic Information Science minor) from Towson University. I believe knowledge is power, and I'm committed to putting environmental data directly into the hands of the local communities it affects most, so they can pursue sustainable solutions on their own terms.

I've verified oil spills for international NGOs pursuing litigation and assessed the environmental impacts of data centers on local water quality at SkyTruth, led urban heat island research at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center, mapped sea level rise and coastal erosion for the Chesapeake Bay Foundation, and produced award-winning research on urban tree canopy equity in Baltimore City.

I'm also rooted in regenerative agriculture — applying GIS to map growing zones and water resources at Sacred Roots Herbal Sanctuary in Shepherdstown, WV, and developing grant funding strategies to support small farms and sustainable land stewardship projects like it.
