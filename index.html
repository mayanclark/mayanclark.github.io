About me

Community advocate,
powered by data.

I'm Maya Clark — a community advocate and geospatial analyst with a B.S. in Geography and Environmental Planning (Geographic Information Science minor) from Towson University. I believe knowledge is power, and I'm committed to putting environmental data directly into the hands of the local communities it affects most, so they can pursue sustainable solutions on their own terms.

My work has spanned nonprofit and government settings alike — from verifying oil spills for international NGOs pursuing litigation and assessing data center impacts on water quality at SkyTruth, to leading a federal research team at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center, to mapping coastal change for the Chesapeake Bay Foundation. Across each, I've brought leadership, vision, and the follow-through to carry an idea from concept to finished product.

That range came together in my own independent research: backed by a $5,000 research grant, I designed, funded, and led a tree canopy equity study in Baltimore City that won a Best Paper award — proof that I can drive a project from first idea to public impact.

I also have a personal connection to regenerative agriculture. I'm currently working to leverage environmental tech to benefit Sacred Roots Herbal Sanctuary in Shepherdstown, WV — including helping map a conservation easement on the property — while also developing a grant program to help sustain the farm's work long-term.
